# Session Five

### Under God's Protection: Godly Discipline and Divine Protection

#### Overview

> For I am with you to save you, declares the Lord. Though I will completely destroy all the nations to which I have scattered you, I will not completely destroy you. Yet I will discipline you justly, and will by no means leave you unpunished.

<< Jeremiah 30:11

> The power for good or evil that resides in a little child is great beyond all human calculation. A child rightly trained may be a worldwide blessing, with an influence reaching onward to eternal years. But a neglected or misdirected child may live to blight and blast mankind, and leave influences of evil which shall roll on in increasing volume till they plunge into the gulf of eternal perdition."

<< George Müller, George Müller of Bristol

The world is a threatening place. Dangers abound. Enemies lurk. Opposition schemes. Trouble menaces. Life in this fallen world can often feel like fierce combat on a hazardous battlefield or an arduous journey in treacherous terrain. We face physical hardship, spiritual attack, emotional turmoil, and relational drama. While we feel this multifaceted peril in our own lives, we also recognize the surrounding danger as we press into our parenting work. To be entrusted with the care of children requires vigilance and attentiveness because of the dangerous surround in which we raise our children. Inherently, parenting a work of protection. Sin will tempt our children onto paths of destruction. Satan will launch attacks against our families. Opponents of Christianity will devalue biblical teaching. Surrounding culture will wage incessant war against the truths and values of the faith. In the midst of dangerous times and hazardous settings, parents are God's appointed protectors of their home. With watchful eye and faithfilled prudence, parents are tasked with defending, guarding, and protecting their home. But, as we will see, parents best fulfill this protector role as they learn to entrust themselves and their children into God's safekeeping.

#### Key Idea

As protectors, we will entrust our family to the God's safekeeping.

#### Introduction

> Thus says the Lord your Redeemer, the Holy One of Israel: "I am the Lord your God, who teaches you for your benefit, who directs you in the way you should go. If only you had paid attention to My commandments, your peace would have been like a river, and your righteousness like waves of the sea.

> Precious, no doubt, are these little ones in your eyes; but if you love them, think often of their souls. No interest should weigh with you so much as their eternal interests. No part of them should be so dear to you as that part which will never die. To pet and pamper and indulge your child, as if this world was all he had to look to, and this life the only season for happiness—to do this is not true love, but cruelty. It is treating him like some beast of the earth, which has but one world to look to, and nothing after death. It is hiding from him that grand truth, which he ought to be made to learn from his very infancy,— that the chief end of his life is the salvation of his soul.

<< J. C. Ryle, The Duties of Parents

Love motivates and energizes parents to be protectors of their children. Like watchmen who guard a city, fathers and mothers are spiritual wardens of their home, protecting their children (and all who enter therein) from danger and threat. As parents, we know that we do not have the strength or wisdom to provide perfect security. This is why the parents first and best act is to entrust their home—themselves, their children, their guests—to God's safekeeping. Under God's watchful eye, a Christian home can become impregnable and unassailable. This does not mean that Christian families are immune to difficulty, pain, or hardship. On the contrary, some Christian homes may experience the greatest attacks and fiercest opponents. Nevertheless, a home under God's protection is most secure, and God will bring each of his children to ultimate safety. Godly discipline is part of a parent's protective work. Parental instruction aims to set children on the paths of life. Parental correction seeks to redirect children off of the paths of destruction. A home under God's protective care takes seriously its duty to raise children in the discipline and instruction of the Lord.

#### Biblical Wisdom

Discipline your son, for in that there is hope; do not be party to his death.

<< Proverbs 19:18

##### Core Principle

Parents are spiritual sentries—guarding their homes by keeping it under God's protective care. Parental discipline is one of the practical ways that parents can establish their home in God's safekeeping. This proverb tells us, "discipline your son, for in that there is hope." For parent and child, discipline can feel inconvenient, uncomfortable, painful, and intrusive. But godly discipline is a sign of genuine love. Biblical discipline sets the child on the path of life. In doing so, parents will "not be party to his death." When we forsake discipline, we make our home porous, allowing any number of danger and sin to enter the home, prey on our children, influence their minds, and subjugate their hearts. In essence, to forsake discipline is to be culpable of our children's destruction. It amounts to leaving door and window ajar, allowing any predator or threat to enter. But we need not be overwhelmed nor overcome by deadly dangers and snares in the world. We have a parental "hope" that gives peace, comfort, and consolation. Entrust your home to the Lord through the practice of godly discipline and make your home a fortress, a refuge, a citadel unto the Lord.

#### A Daring and Dangerous Venture

> I know, O Lord, that a man's way is not his own; no one who walks directs his own steps. Correct me, O Lord, but only with justice—not in Your anger, or You will bring me to nothing.

##### Introduction

The world is filled with danger—physical, spiritual, emotional, and relational. The broken and fractured systems of the world seem to multiply peril and hazard. Sin has corrupted God's good world and turned a garden paradise into a treacherous terrain. Daily, humanity experiences the world in dizzying conflict—evil opposing good, death taunting life, and wickedness seeking to undermine righteousness. In this world, it often seems like evil prevails, the wicked prosper, and enemies thrive. The world has rebelled against its Maker. Godless society is turned against the Lord. Culture is opposed to God. One should expect that, in a fallen world where sin rules as a cruel despot, those things which matter most to the Lord will be under attack. This is the case with the family. <Callout>In his revolt against the Lord, the prince of darkness marks the household as one of his most hated targets.</Callout>

However hostile is this world to the things of God, we need not (we should not!) lose hope. God is the refuge and fortress of his people living in a dangerous land. The church is a haven to the people of God. And, parents are God's appointed protectors over their homes. Psalm 94:1–23 gives us insight how to parent in evil days. This passage will help parents become vigilant in their homebuilding endeavor, raising godly children in a godless age. Helpfully, the psalmist highlights God's loving discipline through which he corrects and instructs his people toward wisdom. Those who heed God's discipline gain rest, security, and blessing. Importantly, God is the great example of all our parenting labors. <Callout>But God is more than just our parenting pattern. He is the safety and security to which all godly families run.</Callout> In the midst of a godless society and prevailing wickedness, building Christian hearth and home involves the work of placing our family in God's safekeeping. This passage that may not seem to offer immediate principles of parenting. But when it is studied on its own terms, we gain a theological vision—a Godcentered way of seeing the world and our parenting work—that serves as a great help in establishing our homes in the Lord.

##### How Long Will the Wicked Exult: Raising Godly Families in a Godless World

The psalm opens by calling out to God as the "God of vengeance" (94:1) and the "Judge of the earth" (94:2). This desperate plea to God is a petition for God to act: to "shine forth" (94:1), to "rise up" (94:2), and to "render a reward" (94:2). Essentially, it is a prayer for vindication: for God to dis- play his justice, act on behalf of his creation, and pay retribution to God's enemies. It is a confession that God's people live in evil times. Since the fall of Eden, God's people have always lived in a wicked world. Each generation of humanity can legitimately say, "The days are dark, the night is prolonged, and the age is perilous." The wicked prowl and the righteous suffer. The righteous psalmist asks for God to shine forth and dispel the darkness, to rise up and administer justice, and to pay back evildoers according to their corrupt ways.

As judge, God opposes those who oppose him. The objects of God's wrath are the "wicked" (94:3; cf. 94:13, 16, 20). The wicked are the "proud" (94:2) and "arrogant" (94:4) people who rejoice in evil (94:4), boast in wrongdoing (94:4), violate God's people (94:5), oppress the needy (94:6), and heedlessly reject God (94:7). They are "workers of iniquity" (94:4; cf. 94:16), who live contrary to God. Their godlessness is proven by their words (94:4, 7), heart intentions (94:2–3), and actions (94:5–6). The righteous psalmist asks God to no longer delay his judgment—"how long" (94:3)—but to act according to his holy and righteous character.

This psalm gives an honest picture of our sinful world. The days are evil.

The times are dangerous. The wicked menace and threaten. We live in a world opposed to God and antagonistic to God's people. Rebellion against God results in hostility to the community of faith. Faithful parenting takes place in dark and evil days. The world is opposed to God, expressing its rebellion in enmity, ill-will, and violence. Godly parents must reckon with the reality that evildoers abound and seek to supplant God's people—and their family—in their defiant rejection of God. Christian parents face many dangers in raising children in godless society. In an evil world, threat to the family can come in the form of physical danger, spiritual attack, mental suffering, emotional turmoil, moral compromise, and relational hardship. This many-sided attack to our homes compels us to develop vigilance and alertness. Few battlegrounds are more fierce than the warfare for our children.

While parenting requires a watchful spirit, it also demands a discerning eye. Some of the greatest threat and danger in our parenting comes from within the sinful human heart. Underneath all godlessness in society is pride (94:2–4)—the exaltation of self against God. all societal corruption comes from the sinful workings of the heart. Pride inevitably turns a person against God, God's people, and the needy (94:5–7). Pride is unprejudicial in who it attacks. In fact, it resides as a poison in every human heart—yes, even the hearts of our youngest children. The exaltation of self, is a subtle but sinister threat that endangers the home. <Callout>While parents raise their children in the midst of a godless society, they also do battle against he depraved and self-promoting human heart.</Callout>

The world is a dangerous place. God has appointed parents as protectors of their home, seeking refuge in God to establish his righteousness. Parents must guard their homes from wickedness and sin—resisting dangers from without and mortifying enemies from within. They must confront falsehood about God and cling to him in truth. They must abandon misplaced pleasure, rejoicing in that which dishonors God. Godly families must embrace a countercultural lifestyle and due diligence to dethrone the fiendish foe of pride. As we protect our homes by the shield of genuine godliness, our families can become protectors of others—to be defenders of the helpless and needy. <Callout>A godly home fosters a sense of justice in its collective consciousness such that it exerts the influence of its countercultural lifestyle on the surrounding world.</Callout> Ultimately, the godly home longs for God to establish justice on the earth. This is their enduring hope.

##### Blessed Is the Man You Discipline, O Lord: Committing to Discipline and Instruction

The second portion of this psalm addresses humanity. It calls people to "take notice" (94:8) and "be wise" (94:8). It is a summons for people to leave their senseless and foolish condition and to become wise and discerning (94:8). In this context, wisdom comes from a foundational truth: God knows all things. He created the ear and hears all (94:9). He made the eye and sees all (94:9). He "knows the thoughts of man, that they are futile" (94:11). God does not "lack knowledge" (94:10). Being all-knowing (omniscient), God instructs humanity: he admonishes, disciplines, and teaches (94:11; cf. 94:12). Contrary to a godless society, God's people are a new humanity in the fallen world. They live faithful to the Lord who sees all, knows all, and understands all. Living before God all-knowing and searching eye, God's people showcase to the world the manifold wisdom of God, a wisdom that comes from living under God's the authority of God's unerring word.

To heed God's instruction is to gain true wisdom, God-given understanding necessary to navigate this perilous world in which we live. Those who heed God's instruction receive God's favor: "blessed is the man You discipline, O Lord, and teach from Your law" (94:12). Those who heed God's word and submit to his discipline are granted "relief from days of trouble" (94:13). The wise and faithful instructed in God's word escape God's wrath, condemnation, and judgment which are reserved for the wicked.

There is a "pit ... dug for the wicked" (94:13), but the righteous will not fall into it. As God's people embrace a countercultural life—a life formed by discipline and instruction in God's word— God promises to "not forsake His people" and "never abandon His heritage" (94:14). God's righteous judgment will be estab- lished. God's justice becomes the code by which the godly live: "the upright in heart will follow it" (94:15).

Discipline and Instruction. Parenting amounts to raising children in the discipline and instruction of the Lord. Godly discipline involves both admonishment (rebuke, chastisement, correction) and teaching (instruction, formation, education). Like all Christian disciples, children need to know the paths of righteousness. They need to be taught and instructed. In the process of this instruction, beliefs and understanding find alignment with God's guiding word. But alignment to the truth can only happen if discipline counterbalances instruction. To know the paths of righteousness is different than walking in these paths. Discipline corrects and reorients children to God's ways when they stray from the path. As God models for us in this psalm, Christian parents must occupy the role of teacher: teaching and correcting, instructing and chastising, educating and rebuking.

Wisdom. To raise children in the discipline and instruction of the world is to cultivate a home of wisdom—calling children out of their senseless simplicity, youthful naiveté, and foolish ways into the ways of prudence and true understanding. The truly wise turn to God. In a godless society where people face the danger of proud evildoers and the pending reality of God's wrath, the safest place to be is among God's people. Wisdom teaches a person to heed these dangers and take refuge in God. God disciplines his people as a means to bring them into wisdom, to preserve them and bless them. The wise who take refuge in God—from godless society and divine wrath—love God's righteous correction and treasure God's eternal truth.

Countercultural Living. The wise who embrace God's word live a countercultural life. They are a contrast to godless society in the way they follow the paths of righteousness. <Callout>The Christian family is designed by God to incubate a countercultural lifestyle of biblical belief, godly living, and holy justice.</Callout> Family life should not only teach children how to navigate the dangers of a godless world; it should teach children how to live such a countercultural life such that they become a compelling witness to the world and a transforming influence on the world. Wise children formed in the family nest—formed by godly instruction and discipline become salt and light to dark and dangerous world.

##### The Lord Has Been My Stronghold: Seeking Divine Protection for Your Home

The psalmist celebrates God as protector. The wicked "band together against the righteous and condemn the innocent to death" (94:20). In the face of powerful systems of evil and wickedness, the righteous person recognizes their own weakness. At the foundation of their life and faith, God's people recognize their own frailty: their "foot is slipping" (94:18). They know that they pending death—"the abode of silence" (94:17). They are barraged by "anxiety [that] overwhelms" (94:19). All people are fragile in the face of human wickedness and divine wrath. But the righteous person finds safe haven in the Lord. He trusts God as his "helper" (94:17). He clings to God's "loving devotion" to support him (94:18). He rests in God's "consolation" (94:19) as a delight to his soul. For the righteous, God is a "stronghold" and "rock of refuge" (94:22). In fact, God is a conquering warrior who ultimately defeats the wicked. Speaking of the wicked, the psalm testifies that God brings "upon them their own iniquity" and destroys them "for their wickedness" (94:23). God is the one who rises up against the wicked and stands against the evildoers (94:16). God does not ally himself with the wicked (94:20). Instead, he is the guard and defense of his people.

In his power and faithfulness, God protects his people. <Callout>While parents need spiritual vigilance in order to rightly parent their children in a godless society, they also need childlike faith to entrust themselves to God's safekeeping.</Callout> The psalm that began in a plea for justice concludes with a confession of trust. The best thing a parent can do is the entrust its family to God's loving care. God will watch over, guard, and protect our children better than we can ever do. Parents best discharge their duty as protectors when they lead their home to trust God as stronghold and refuge. When God is our shelter and defense, we enjoy his loving devotion and fatherly comfort. To teach children how to find safety in God is foundational to all other parenting work. In fact, this rudimentary lesson is essential for our discipline plan to have godly effect. All godly correction and instruction is about making God our safety and security.

##### Conclusion

The battle for hearth and home is where spiritual combat is often most fierce. The enemy knows that if he can destroy the home, he can corrupt society, afflict the church, bruise the believer, revile the gospel, and oppose thew works of God. We all parent at a time when the days are evil. The fallen world presents a most challenging backdrop in which we must raise our children. But God is not surprised. Vigilant parents who seek for God's justice will submit themselves to God's disciplining hand. They will receive correction and instruction with humble faith. As God disciplines them, they will discipline their children—in love, patience, truth, and righteousness. Their discipline will be oriented to building wisdom and discernment. Further, parents will seek God as their ultimate security. They will lead their family by placing it under God's protective care. <Callout>Christian homes that are secure in God's stronghold can in turn become a safe haven for the needy and helpless around them.</Callout> Let's accept the challenge to raise our families with loving discipline so that they can be instruments of righteousness and justice in a evil and depraved world.

#### Session Summary

- We parent in evil days. The world is filled with danger and peril. Spiritual enemies seek to destroy families, attacking parents and children. Families face enemies from without (rebellious godless society) and within (proud sinful hearts)
- Godly discipline is an act of love toward our children. Godly discipline should be both instructive and corrective. Discipline is a way that parents act as protectors and guardians of their home.
- Godly discipline and instruction aims to cultivate wisdom and understanding. Biblical wisdom helps teaches children how to live skillfully in the world in a countercultural way that will be a witness of God's truth and a transforming influence in the world.
- Godly parents direct their children to trust in God. Parenting work involves cultivating faith in God's protective care: to not hide from God but to hide in God. Christian home are marked by God's protective care. Godly parents entrust their homes to God's safekeeping.

We surrender our hearth and home to you. As parents, we are your appointed watchmen over our children. Make us vigilant and aware. Keep us awake and alert. Grow us in grace and godliness. #### Project Explanation

#### Family Discipline Plan

In this project, you and your spouse will outline a discipline plan for your family. You should consider the underlying convictions and principles that support your discipline goals as well as give concrete expressions to rules and strategies for raising your children in the faith. This should all result in a succinct family code that defines the discipline of the house.

- **Family Convictions**: The timeless, biblical truths that guide our house discipline.
- **Wisdom Principles**: The wisdom teachings that inform our approach to discipline.
- **House Rules**: The basic guidelines for conduct and family life that define and govern our home (general enough to apply to any circumstance).
- **Discipline Strategies**: The means by which you will restoratively correct and formatively instruct.
- **Family Code**: The rule of life or code of ethic that defines and orders your family life.
