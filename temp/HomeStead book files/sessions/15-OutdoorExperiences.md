# Outdoor Experiences

### HomeSteading: Practicing Parental Duty in the Outdoors

#### Overview

> Therefore everyone who hears these words of Mine and acts on them is like a wise man who built his house on the rock. The rain fell, the torrents raged, and the winds blew and beat against that house; yet it did not fall, because its foundation was on the rock. But everyone who hears these words of Mine and does not act on them is like a foolish man who built his house on sand. The rain fell, the torrents raged, and the winds blew and beat against that house, and it fell—and great was its collapse!

<< Matthew 7:24–27

> In some points of view the community is made up of the individuals whom it includes. The population of a city is the number of separate persons the city contains. But on the side of its moral character, the community is made up of the families within it; and what they are, as to purity, order, and religion, the community will be. In most great cities there are districts in which the families are few in comparison with the number of individuals. Such districts are commonly the haunts of criminals, and even the value of the property is usually lowered as the result. In such places there are houses but not homes, dwellings but not families.

<< John Hall, *A Christian Home*

This section of the rite of passage will help reflect on your duty as a godly parent—building Christian hearth and home—through outdoor experiences. These challenges are meant to be embodied experiences in the outdoors that give you time to consider the spiritual responsibility of parenting through a physical outdoor activity. Some may consider these challenges unnecessary. They seek merely the instructions of godly parenting and hope to implement them as soon as possibly. This may be well and good. But the ability to have experiences that affect the physical senses can strengthen our commitment and understanding of our spiritual duties. These activities were also designed with the church in mind. As parents go through these experienced together, they hopefully will create memories, establish bonds, and fortify relationships with each other. Not only does being out in creation help us commune with our Lord, it also helps forge relationships with one another, helping us to become a mature church that operates as a parenting community.

## Outdoor Experience One

### Homemaking

*This outdoor experience is intended to connect an experience in nature with our parenting duty. As you go through this experience, do so reflectively, considering how it parallels with and gives insight into parenting duty.*

#### Inspiration: Getting the Right Mindset

*Read the following before you perform this outdoor activity.*

<< But everything must be done in a proper and orderly manner.

<< 1 Corinthians 14:40

> Families are nurseries of all societies ... Well-ordered families naturally produce a good order in other societies.

<< Cotton Mather, *A Family Well-Ordered*

The house represents the family—its present life, past lineage, and future legacy. Christian parenting involves establishing home according to God's design. Order in a home fosters peace, harmony, stability, and maturity. In a well-ordered home, we cultivate a context in which our children—and our guests—can grow in the gospel and in God's grace. The physical activity of setting up camp will help you reflect on the spiritual duty of ordering your home.

#### Implementation: Fulfilling Our Family Duty

*Follow the directions below to perform this outdoor activity.*

Christian homebuilding seeks to establish one's family according to God's order and design. God's word is a blueprint for our families. We need a vision for what a well-ordered home looks like. In this outdoor activity, you will set up your campsite for your family—an act of homemaking. This physical act will help you reflectively consider your spiritual duty to your family. Remember: In the act of setting up camp, you are not just preparing for an outdoor family camp experience—you are practicing the sacred and solemn work of building Christian hearth and home. The following tasks will guide you in this embodied activity:

- Gather and organize supplies for the campsite.
- Set up the tent and sleeping areas.
- Delineate areas for cooking, play, rest, and work.
- Define the boundaries of the campsite.

## Experience Two: Hearth Building

### Hearth Building

*This outdoor experience is intended to connect an experience in nature with our parenting duty. As you go through this experience, do so reflectively, considering how it parallels with and gives insight into parenting duty.*

#### Inspiration: Getting the Right Mindset

*Read the following before you perform this outdoor activity.*

<< A friend loves at all times, and a brother is born for adversity.

<< Proverbs 17:17

> The Christian has joy as other men have in the common mercies of life ... He is not dead to the happiness of the household: around his hearth he finds happy associations, without which life were drear indeed. His children fill his home with glee, his wife is his solace and delight, his friends are his comfort and refreshment.

<< C. H. Spurgeon, "A Bundle of Myrrh"

The hearth signifies the intimate bonds and daily provision experienced with the family. Hearts bond around a family hearth. The warmth of the fire reminds us of the comfort of close and trusted relationships. The fire as fuel reminds us of God's gracious provision—food and fellowship, health and holiness, responsibility and refreshment. At the hearth, one enjoys the intimate conversation with kin and close relations. Song and jubilee can often be heard around a simple, community-building fire. To build a Christian home, we must tend to our household hearth—keeping the bonds of our relationship with one another fresh and fertile.

#### Implementation: Fulfilling Our Family Duty

*Follow the directions below to perform this outdoor activity.*

Christian homebuilding seeks to cultivate deep and lasting bonds with one another. The relationships we form in our families shape the way we approach relationships for the rest of our lives. The family is a fertile context for fellowship and communion. In this outdoor activity, you will set up your fire pit for your family—an act of hearth building. This physical act will help you reflectively consider your spiritual duty to your family. Remember: In the act of setting up your hearth, you are not just preparing for an outdoor family camp experience—you are practicing the sacred and solemn work of building a family culture that nurtures and nourishes. The following tasks will guide you in this embodied activity:

- Gather stones to demarcate the fire area.

- Establish any cook areas over the fire.
- Set up sitting areas for people to fellowship.
- Make sure that the firepit is safe and the fire will not spread.
- Start the fire and keep it going during the trip.

## Experience Three: Shepherd’s Carry

### Shepherd's Carry

*This outdoor experience is intended to connect an experience in nature with our parenting duty. As you go through this experience, do so reflectively, considering how it parallels with and gives insight into parenting duty.*

#### Inspiration: Getting the Right Mindset

*Read the following before you perform this outdoor activity.*

> Save Your people and bless Your inheritance; shepherd them and carry them forever.

<< Psalm 28:9

> Let it be the principal part of your care and labour ... to make holiness appear to them the most necessary, honourable, gainful, pleasant, delightful, amiable state of life ... Especially draw them to the love of it, by representing it as lovely ... Yet in putting them upon the practice of religious duties, you must carry them on by degrees, and put them at first upon no more than they can bear.

<< Richard Baxter, *The Duties of Parents*

Parenting is simultaneously a gracious gift and weighty responsibility. Alongside the joy of family life is the sacred duty to raise children in the faith. This is shepherding work. The shepherd signifies a caretaker who bears responsibility to feed, nourish, protect, and guide the flock. A good shepherd puts the needs of the flock before his own and is willing to lay down his life for the sheep. Parents must learn to shepherd their children, discharging this duty with a trust in God's shepherding care over them. To build a Christian home, we must learn to bear parental responsibility with sobriety, joy, and dependence upon the Lord.

#### Implementation: Fulfilling Our Family Duty

*Follow the directions below to perform this outdoor activity.*

Christian homebuilding tends to the family like a flock. Ultimately, Christian parents recognize that their children belong to God most of all. To raise a child in the faith is a heavy duty on parental shoulders. In this outdoor activity, you will go on a hike with your family with a weighted backpack—an act of bearing responsibility on behalf of your family and children. This physical act will help you reflectively consider your spiritual duty to your family. Remember: In the act of carrying a heavy load, you are not just testing your physical stamina and strength—you are practicing the sacred and solemn work of tending to your children like a good shepherd. The following tasks will guide you in this embodied activity:

- Plan a family hike with all the members of your family.

- Fill a backpack with weight (either rocks or supplies).
- Hike together as a family, willingly adding the other supplies of others in your own pack.
- As you hike, consider the spiritual, God-given duty given to you as a parent.

## Experience Four: Table Setting

### Table Setting

*This outdoor experience is intended to connect an experience in nature with our parenting duty. As you go through this experience, do so reflectively, considering how it parallels with and gives insight into parenting duty.*

#### Inspiration: Getting the Right Mindset

*Read the following before you perform this outdoor activity.*

> Continue in brotherly love. Do not neglect to show hospitality to strangers, for by so doing some people have entertained angels without knowing it.

<< Hebrews 13:1–2

> Welcome poor men and strangers to your homely board, that with them Christ may be your guest.

<< Jerome, *Letters*

The table is central to a Christian home. Around the table, families enjoy food, express fellowship, foster conversation, and offer encouragement. During meals, members of a family strengthen the deep bonds of their relationships. Friendships are built and God is explored. Life is investigated and belonging is realized. Joys are shared and sorrows are shouldered. At the table, families welcome guests to participate in their household life—caring for the poor, tending to the needy, and welcoming the marginalized. Godly parents learn to use table fellowship as one of its most sacred and powerful instruments in building Christian hearth and home.

#### Implementation: Fulfilling Our Family Duty

*Follow the directions below to perform this outdoor activity.*

Christian homebuilding sets the table for close bonds of relationship and fellowship. The togetherness experienced around the table is profound. Setting a welcoming table is crucial to cultivate a healthy home. It is also vital means of embodying gospel truth. In our families, we should welcome others as God has welcomed us. In this outdoor activity, you will make a meal for your family and extend the invitation to others to join in—an act of making your family inviting and hospitable. This physical act will help you reflectively consider your spiritual duty to your family. Remember: In the act of setting the table, you are not just feeding stomachs and nourishing bodies—you are knitting souls together in a way that can represent Christ. The following tasks will guide you in this embodied activity:

- Plan out a meal and gather the supplies.
- Give everyone in your family a task for preparation.
- If you are camping with other families, invite these families to the meal.
- Organize the meal time to be one that intentionally fosters relationship and cultivates a welcoming atmosphere. Consider how you will open the meal, prayers you will pray, blessings you will offer, and topics you will discuss.

## Experience Five: Night Vigil

### Night Vigil

<Question id="Home-ParTwoCha-OutExp-ExpFivNigVig-NigVig-1">*Write a final prayer to consecrate your home to the Lord.*</Question>

*This outdoor experience is intended to connect an experience in nature with our parenting duty. As you go through this experience, do so reflectively, considering how it parallels with and gives insight into parenting duty.*

#### Inspiration: Getting the Right Mindset

*Read the following before you perform this outdoor activity.*

> Then the lookout a shouted: "Day after day, my lord, I stand on the watchtower; night after night I stay at my post."

<< Isaiah 21:8

> The scope of our art is to provide the soul with wings, to rescue it from the world and give it to God, and to watch over that which is in His image, if it abides, to take it by the hand, if it is in danger, or restore it, if ruined, to make Christ to dwell in the heart by the Spirit.

<< Gregory Nazianzen, *Orations*

Because the family is one of God's greatest gifts, it is the one of the enemy's most hated targets. Faced with temptation, sin, affliction, and opposition, the family is under fierce attack. Parents are to remain watchful over their families. While the family sleeps, we should remain alert, circumspect, and vigilant. God has entrusted the safety of our home to our watchful eye. Although God is the ultimate protector of our home, we are to exercise faithful guardianship—filtering the influences that come into our home, managing the culture within our home, and mobilizing the good work done outside our home. While a spiritual war rages around us, we must—like watchmen—remain attentive, heedful, observant, and wise. Sometimes, God may even awaken us in the midnight hours to lather our family in protective prayer.

#### Implementation: Fulfilling Our Family Duty

*Follow the directions below to perform this outdoor activity.*

Christian homebuilding involves guarding our family. Parents are God-appointed watchmen over their home. To fulfill this duty, we must develop a watchful eye, strengthen an alert mind, cultivate spiritual discernment, and sharpen our attentive spirit. In this outdoor activity, you will perform a night vigil—an act of watching over your family with prayerfulness and vigilance. This physical act will help you reflectively consider your spiritual duty to your family. Remember: In the act of keeping watch at night, you are not just guarding tents and campsites—you practicing what it means to remain vigilant at all times, knowing that our greatest and most watchful act is to entrust our family tot he Lord's safekeeping. The following tasks will guide you in this embodied activity:

- Choose a night to keep guard over your family. Identify which hours of the night you will keep vigil.
- During the vigil, reflect on the dangers that present themselves to your family in the surrounding world. Reflect on your God-appointed duty to protect them.
- During the vigil, write a prayer of protection over your family. Be specific about the safety and protection needed to care for your family well.
- If you are camping as a group, consider how to keep every hour of night guarded by taking shifts as a community.
